Kandy

Temple of the Tooth Relic

Temple of the tooth relic is perhaps the most religious place you could visit in Sri Lanka. The Buddha's tooth relic has been enshrined in this temple. Buddhists from all over the world visit this place to pay homage to the tooth relic by accepting them as the Buddha himself. Temple of tooth also displays a unique type of architecture such as the Paththiripppuwa which is of the main design.

Royal Palace of Kandy

Towards the North of the temple of tooth lies the Royal palace of Kandy built by king Vickramabahu III. This palace was the royal residence of the monarchs who lived during the Kandyan era. The royal palace consists of many parts such as Raja Wasala, Maha Maluwa, Magul Maduwa, Wadahindina Mandappe, Palle Vahale, Meda Wasala, Ran Ayuda Maduwa and Ulpange.

Lankatilaka Vihara

The Lankatilaka Vihara is located in Udunuwara of Kandy. This temple was built by Buwanekabahu IV during the 14 th century. The buildings of this temple has been built on a rock called Panhalgala rock. Many rock curved inscriptions can be found in this temple which have been written in both Sinhala and Tamil.

Gadaladeniya Viharaya

Gadaladeniya temple built by King Buwanekabahu IV is located in Pilimathalawa of Kandy. Gadaladeniya temple is one of the largest rock temples in Sri Lanka. The speciality of this temple is that its architecture resembles that of Hindus.

Royal botanical garden peradeniya

The royal botanical garden of Kandy is a magnificent paradise of flowers and plants. This garden was grown during the 14th Century. The longest river in Sri Lanka, river Mahaweli flows through this park. The garden consistes of many sections such as a Bamboo forest, a lake, a bridge, an orchid collection and an avenue of palm trees.
