Batticaloa Lagoon

Batticaloa lagoon is one of most visited places when it comes tourism in Sri Lanka. Batticaloa lagoon is 56 km long making it one of the biggest lagoons in the country. It extends from Eravur in the Batticaloa district to Kalmunai in the Ampara district. This massive lagoon is home to a vast diversity of flaura and fauna. A maginficent view of the ocean could be seen through this lagoon.

Kalkudah and Pasikudah

Kalkudah and Pasikudah are 2 coastal resort towns located 35 from Batticoloa. Pasikudah has one of the most stretched shallow coastal reef in the world. Both Kalkudah and Pasikudah used to popular tourist destination in Sri Lanka but when the 2004 Tsunami incident occured and due the prevailing civil war at that time, the fame held by Kalkudah and Pasikudah declined rapidly. Subsequently in the later years these 2 towns have reached back to their fame and currently is one of the most travelled places in Sri Lanka.
