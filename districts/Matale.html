Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also considered as the 8 th wonder of the world is an ancient rock fortress. This fortress has been built on top a massive rock which is of 180m in height. Sigirya Fortress was built by king Kashyapa. The entrance of the fortress is shaped like a lion. There are fountains in the fortress even modern archeologists have no idea how the water rises from 180m to the top of the rock depicting the highly advanced architectural skills of ancient Sri Lankans. Sigiriya has been listed by the UNESCO as a world heritage site.

Dambulla Cave Temple

The Dambulla cave temple a.k.a Golden Temple of Dambulla was declared a world heritage site in 1991. This cave temple is considered as the largest and best preserved cave temple in the country. The temple is surrounded by 80 caves. Paintings and statues depicting the life of the Buddha can be seen here.

Aluvihare Rock Temple

Aluvihare rock temple is another one of the ancient Sri Lankan temples located in Aluvihare in Matale. It is believed that King Devanampiyatissa built the Aluvihare pagoda and that this temple was built surrounding this stupa.

Knuckles Mountain Range

The Knuckles mountain range a.k.a Dumbara mountain range is a trekker's paradise. The mountains peak is covered with a thick envelope of mist. Sri Lanka's longest river i.e. River Mahaweli flows through this mountain range. The Knuckles Mountain range is considered as a world heritage site.
