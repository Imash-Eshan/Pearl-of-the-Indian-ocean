The Parakrama Samudraya is perhaps the great triumph of king Parakramabahu.Its name suggests 'the ocean of King parakramabahu' due to its massive size. Nissanka Latha Mandapaya

Nissanka Latha Mandapaya located in Polonnaruwa was built by King Nissanka Malla and was named after him. According to ancient inscriptions, it was this building the king used to come in to listen to sermons. The structure is mainly made out of granite. According to archeologists, the stone colomn in Nissanka Latha Mandapaya is the best example to depict ancient Sri Lankan architecture.

Vatadage

The Vatadage resembles a combination of both Indian and Sri Lankan architecture. Vatadage is a protective structure built around small stupas. They may have wooden roofs held on stone colomns. There are only very few Vatadges left in Sri Lanka out of which the one in Polonnaruwa holds a special place

Minneriya national park

The present Minneriya National park was originally created to become a wildlife sanctuary. There is a vast diversity of flaura and fauna in this park;27 species of fish, 25 species of mammals, 165 species of birds, 75 species of butterflies, 11 species of amphibians and 22 species of reptiles. Though this place is a home to a vast number of animal species, Minneriya national park is mostly famous for its elephants.

Palace of King Parakramabahu

The palace used to be consisting of 7 stories and 1000 chambers which displays the highly advanced engineering architecture of ancient Sri Lankans. Due to Chola invasions the palace got heavily deteriorated and at present the ruins are the remaining which can be seen.
