Kurunegala

The Silver Temple

The silver Temple a.k.a Ridi Viharaya is a small cave Temple built by King Dutugemunu during the 2nd century BC. The silver temple consistes of many sections; The main rock-cut temple, Lower Temple, Pancha Nari Gataya (The knot of five maidens) in the lower temple, Upper Temple, an Artificial lake etc

Yapahuwa Rock Fortress

Similar to Sigirya, Yapahuwa rock fortress is also a fortress built on a massive rock. The rock which the fortress has been built on rises 90m high to the sky. This fortress is one of the best archeological sites in Sri Lanka. Experts suggest that the best time to visit Yapahuwa is in the morning.

Athugala - The Elephant Rock

Athugala a.k.a the elephant rock is a majestic rock that overlooks the entire city of Kurunegala. This rock was given its name due its shape resembling that of an elephant. This massive rock rises to 325 m above ground. There is a massive statue of the Buddha built on top of the rock which further heightens its majesticity.

Panduwasnuwara

Panduwasnuwara is an ancient city situated in Kurunegala. During the reign of King Pandukabhaya, this city used to be the capital of Sri Lanka. There are many ancient ruins, which dates back to King Pandukabhaya's reign in Panduwasnuwara such as the Ektamgeya(The tower where King Pandukabhaya's mother, Unmada Chithra was kept imprisoned).
