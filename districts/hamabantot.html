Yala National Park

Yala National park is the 2nd largest national park in the country. Its has a reputation due to it being the most visited national park in Sri Lanka. It covers a total area of 979 square Kilometers. Yala national park houses to a massive variety of exotic flaura and fauna. This is also a sanctuary for elephants, leopards, bears and birds.

Madunagala Hot Springs

Madunagala Hot Springs/Madunagala hot wells is a highly visited place in Hambanatota. This has gotten its reputation due to the prescence of 'healing water' which has been explained by science stating that these wells have water which consists of 22 minerals. There are 6 wells that can be used in public.

Bird Resort Hambantota

Bird Resort Hambantota is Asia's largest bird park and research centre. This bird park houses a variety of species of birds(over 180) including endemic birds. There are over 3200 birds here. The park is about 35 acres in area. This place could be paradise for ornithologists.

Walawe river

Walawe river is one of the longest rivers in Sri Lanka accounting to a length of 138 km. It starts from the infamous Adam's peak and goes all the way to the ocean.

Sithulpawwa Buddhist Monastery

Sithulpawwa was built by King Kavantissa somewhere during the 2nd Century BC. Buddhist devotees from all around the world visit this place to worship. Many ancients painting and ruins can be seen here.
